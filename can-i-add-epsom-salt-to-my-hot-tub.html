Can I add Epsom salt to my hot tub?

February 03, 2024

It is not okay to put Epsom salts in a hot tub. They can cause damage and destroy your hot tub, costing you thousands in repair or replacement. Epsom salts are a chemical composition called Magnesium Sulphate, which is an Alkaline chemical compound.

What happens if you put Epsom salt in a hot tub?

While Epsom salt in the bathtub is OK, hot tubs and Epsom salt simply aren't a good combination. Pure Epsom salt has acidic properties that can disrupt the pH balance and total alkalinity of the water in your hot tub, which can eventually cause your tub equipment to corrode.

How much Epsom salt should I put in my hot tub?

The average bathtub holds about 80 gallons of water. On the other hand, the average hot tub holds about 400-500 gallons. To reach the same concentration level in your hot tub would require 10-12 cups of pure Epsom salts. That's a lot of solids in the water!

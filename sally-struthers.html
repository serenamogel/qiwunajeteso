Sally Struthers Biography

Sally Struthers is an American actress, activist, and spokesperson. She is well known for playing Gloria Stivic’s role in ABC’s sitcom’ All in the Family,’ for which she won two Emmy Awards. Sally began her career in the early 1970s and quickly moved into stardom. She then gained further success not only in films and on television, but also on stage.

A surgeon’s daughter, Struthers graduated from Grant High School and later studied acting at the Pasadena Playhouse College of Theater Arts, earning the title of the institution’s “most promising student.” In 1977, the actress married psychiatrist William C. Rader, with whom she divided ways after having a child. Besides being an actress, Struthers has also served as a spokesperson for many organizations, including the Christian Children’s Fund, asking for help from the public to end hunger in underdeveloped nations, especially those in Africa.

Sally Struthers Husband| Daughter

Sally Struthers is reportedly single at present but, previously she was married to William C. Rader, an American psychiatrist. The duo tied the knot on December 18, 1977, at an intimate ceremony in the presence of their closed ones and family members.

Struthers’s husband Rader co-wrote one of the episodes of her series, All in the Family in 1977 and also involved in her negotiations with TV executives. The pair shares a daughter, Samantha Rader who graduated with a degree in Cognitive Science in 2001 from Vassar College.

The couple divorced on January 18, 1983, in Los Angeles. However, after divorce, they still keep-in-touch with one another.

Sally Struthers  Career

Sally Struthers starred in the theatrical production of’ Grease’ at the Eugene O’Neill Theatre from 1994 to 1998. Soon after that, she appeared as Miss Hannigan in the play’ Annie’ at the Fabulous Fox Theater and later in her national tour. The actress has been a regular at the Ogunquit Playhouse since the early 2000s.

She featured in a number of plays in the early 2010s including’ Love Letters,” Curtains,” Cinderella,” Hello, Dolly,’ and’ The Drowsy Chaperone.’ Struthers performed in’ 9 to 5 as Roz Klein: The Musical’ in 2012. She played the role of Agnes Gooch in’ Mame’ at the Riverside Center Dinner Theatre from 2015 to 2016.

Sally Struthers Film and television career

Struthers first played a small but memorable role in the 1970 drama film ‘ Five Easy Pieces’ in which she appeared opposite Jack Nicholson. She also starred in the TV shows’ The Smothers Brothers Comedy Hour’ and’ The Tim Conway Comedy Hour’ that year. She joined the sitcom’ All in the Family’ cast a year later as Gloria Stivic, a role she played until 1978.

The actress featured as a veterinarian’s restless wife in’ The Getaway,’ a hit in the 1972 box office. She played a victim of physical abuse in the TV film ‘ Intimate Strangers’ in 1977. From 1982 to 1983, Struthers took over Gloria’s role in Archie Bunker’s Place spin-off series ‘Gloria.’ She then joined as an occasional guest the show ‘ Win, Lose or Draw.’

She was cast in the regular role of Babette Dell in ‘Gilmore Girls’ in 2000. Three years later, Struthers started playing Louise Miller, the manipulative mother of Bill Miller in the ‘Still Standing’ television series. She also provided voices for several animated series including ‘The Pebbles and Bamm-Bamm Show,’ ‘Fred Flintstone and Friends,’ ‘TaleSpin,’ ‘Dinosaurs,’ ‘Droopy, Master Detective’ and ‘Betsy’s Kindergarten Adventures,’ to name a few.

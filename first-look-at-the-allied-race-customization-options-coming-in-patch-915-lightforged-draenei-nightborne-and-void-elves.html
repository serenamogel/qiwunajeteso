First Look At The Allied Race Customization Options Coming In Patch 9.1.5

After last week's announcement that Blizzard will be adding more Allied Race customization options in patch 9.1.5, they have released the first look at some of the new Lightforged Draenei and Nighborne updates.

After Blizzard had initially said there would be no more customization options added in the Shadowlands, there was enough player feedback that prompted Blizzard to act and begin designing new customization for players.

Blizzard had also stated that there would be new druid travel forms added, however, there is no new information to share.

The Nightborne were heavily criticized when they were released, as they were different than the NPC models in the game.

With the new updates, the face has been adjusted, with more options to change the eyes, as well as, more jewelry available.

Blizzard also released some early designs of the updated Lightforged Draenei, in which we can see some new hairstyles, colors, tattoos, or jewelry on their backs, and updates to their tails.

Blizzard had also announced after the initial post that Void Elves would be receiving 5 new hairstyles for players to customize at the barbershop.

There is currently no date announced for 9.1.5, and apart from the new customization options, the only announced information is the addition of solo Island Expeditions.

With Classic Burning Crusade phase 2 expected to release in the next month, we are likely to see a release date for Shadowlands patch 9.1.5 after that goes live.

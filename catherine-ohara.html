Catherine Anne O’ Hara is a Canadian actress. She is known for being a multi-talented star who is a great comedian and writer. Having good writing and comic skills, she turned her passion into a career. She is the recipient of many awards including the Screen Actors Guild Awards, Canadian Screen Awards, Primetime Emmy Awards, and many others.

In 2018, Catherine O’ Hara was selected as an Officer of the Order of Canada. She was honored with the Governor General’s Performing Arts Lifetime Artistic Achievement Award in 2020.

Career

In 1974, Catherine O’ Hara drew attention after giving her first performance, when she was a member of ‘The Second City’ improvisational comedy troupe in Toronto.

The actress appeared in her first television sitcom show named ‘Coming Up Rosie’, started in 1975 and ended in 1978. In the series, she acted alongside John Candy and Dan Aykroyd.

In 1976, she then made an appearance in the sketch comedy series named ‘Second City Television’. The series was ended in the year 1984. For her performance, she got huge acclaim. Her work as an actress and writer was truly appreciated. And this led her to win a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Variety Series in 1981.

However, Catherine O’ Hara did many films and got prominence for her performances. She has the ability to win the hearts of many with her admiring acting prowess.

Achievements

Genie Award, a Golden Globe Award, two Screen Actors Guild Awards, two Primetime Emmy Awards, and six Canadian Screen Awards are some of the major achievements of Catherine’s life.
